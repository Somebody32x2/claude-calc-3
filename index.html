Calc 3 · Field Labs

Multivariable, felt

Interactive labs for the core ideas of Calculus III. Every surface, slope and volume on these pages is computed from an exact closed form — nothing is faked, so the numbers you read are the real ones. Drag, slide, and predict before you look.

Lab 03

Directional derivatives

Du f = ∇f · u

Any compass heading, not just the two axes. A rotating cutting plane, the circle every direction's slope traces, and why level curves run perpendicular to the gradient.

Lab 04

Tangent planes

L(x,y) ≈ f(x,y)

Zoom in until surface and plane are indistinguishable — local linearity, made visible. The error shrinks as the square of the distance, and the differential turns that into estimates.

Lab 05

Hills, valleys & saddles

D = fxxfyy − fxy²

Where the ground goes flat, and how the discriminant sorts each flat spot into peak, pit, or pass. A gradient field, the Hessian's axes, and Lagrange multipliers to close.

Lab 06

Polar & the Jacobian

dA = r dr dθ

Why a polar tile carries an extra r. Rings and rays, the sector that becomes r dr dθ, the Jacobian as a live area-stretch factor, and the Gaussian integral it unlocks.

Lab 07

Triple integrals

dV = ρ² sin φ dρ dφ dθ

The same slicing logic one dimension up. A sweeping blade through a solid, the cylindrical cell, and where the ρ² sin φ actually comes from — watch the latitude circle shrink.
